The Nicotine Ladder of Health

Understanding the safest and most harmful ways to consume nicotine

Smoking harms because of combustion, not nicotine. The ladder below shows safer alternatives step by step, from highest harm to lowest harm.

Level 1: Combustible Cigarettes

Cigarettes are the most harmful way to consume nicotine. Burning tobacco produces more than 7000 chemicals, including tar and carbon monoxide. Fifty percent of long term smokers die from smoking related diseases.

Level 2: Heated Tobacco

Heated tobacco warms tobacco to around 350C without burning it. This removes combustion and reduces harmful chemicals by up to 95 percent. These products still contain tobacco but eliminate tar and carbon monoxide.

Level 3: UK Regulated Vapes and Nicotine Pouches

UK regulated vapes use pharmaceutical grade nicotine and do not contain tobacco. These products are highly regulated by UK law and are significantly safer than combustion.

Level 4: Nicotine Replacement Therapy

These treatments include gum, patches, lozenges, sprays, and inhalators. They contain pharmaceutical grade nicotine and are clinically tested to help people reduce or stop smoking.
